Endangered Species

An endangered species is a type of organism that is threatened with extinction due to various human activities and natural factors. According to the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), a species is considered endangered when it meets one or more of the following criteria:

Population decline: A significant decline in population size or range over a specified period (usually 3-5 generations).

Small population size: A population size of fewer than 2,500 mature individuals, or a population size of fewer than 250 mature individuals with a declining trend.

Restricted range: A species with a very small or fragmented range, making it vulnerable to extinction due to habitat loss or degradation.
